# MBC Survey 2025 — Social Values Analysis
**Data:** 432 respondents (36 questions × 6 age groups × 2 genders)
**Scale:** 1 = Strongly Disagree · 3 = Neutral · 5 = Strongly Agree
**Groups:** 20s–70s × Male/Female

---

## 1. Landscape Overview

### Most Widely Agreed-Upon Values (cross-group consensus)

| Score | Statement |
|-------|-----------|
| 4.12 | We must leave a better world for the next generation |
| 4.12 | Fair treatment should be the first principle of legislation |
| 4.01 | Caring for the weak and vulnerable is important |
| 3.95 | Today's society is too sensitive about language |
| 3.93 | Punishing wrongdoers feels satisfying |

> **Note:** Despite surface-level political divides, Koreans across all demographics share a strong commitment to legal fairness and intergenerational responsibility — while also broadly agreeing that public discourse has become *too* sensitive.

### Least Agreed-Upon (near-universal rejection)

| Score | Statement |
|-------|-----------|
| 1.74 | I feel a thrill at natural disasters in other countries |
| 1.75 | I think society needs to completely collapse |
| 1.78 | I feel the urge to destroy beautiful things |
| 1.99 | I imagine most of humanity disappearing |

> Destructive and nihilistic impulses are universally low across all groups — though notably less so among younger males (see §3).

## 2. Generational Divide (20s vs. 70s)

The largest generational gaps cluster around **gender roles and family structure**.

| Gap (70s − 20s) | Direction | Statement |
|-----------------|-----------|-----------|
| **+1.33** | Older ↑ | Having children is a social obligation |
| **+1.10** | Older ↑ | Women should prioritize family and children |
| **+0.99** | Older ↑ | Children suffer when mothers work |
| **+0.56** | Older ↑ | We need a strong leader to suppress extremism |
| **+0.56** | Older ↑ | Compassion and sympathy are important virtues |
| **−0.52** | Younger ↑ | Going abroad instead of ancestral rites is acceptable |
| **−0.46** | Younger ↑ | Marriage is a personal choice |
| **−0.46** | Younger ↑ | Success sometimes requires pushing others down |

### Interpretation

- **Traditional family roles** are a uniquely sharp generational fault line. Younger Koreans (especially women) have largely abandoned the expectation that motherhood and career are in conflict.
- Younger generations are **more individualistic** about marriage and personal success strategies, but *not* more permissive about destructive behavior.
- Older generations score *higher* on abstract pro-social values (compassion, equality, fairness) — suggesting these values are not a youth phenomenon but a life-course one.

## 4. Group Profiles

### 20s Male — "Anti-PC, Meritocratic, Frustrated"

**Highest agreement:**
- Today's society is too sensitive about language (**4.02**) ← highest among all groups
- Punishing wrongdoers feels satisfying (3.92)
- Fair treatment should be the first principle of legislation (3.88)

**Lowest agreement:**
- Foreign natural disaster thrill (1.86)
- Urge to destroy beautiful things (1.99)
- Society needs to completely collapse (2.06)

**Key characteristics:**
- Strongest "anti-PC" sentiment of any group — well above even 20s females (3.93)
- Scores the gender wage gap as having legitimate reasons at **3.31**, vs. 20s females at **2.25** (gap: +1.06) — the largest single intra-cohort gender gap in the survey
- Relatively skeptical of redistribution: "everyone earns the same" scores only 2.33
- Does *not* show elevated destructive impulses despite frustration framing

**Implied narrative:** 20s Korean men feel the rules of fairness are not being applied consistently, resent what they perceive as preferential treatment of vulnerable groups, and express this primarily through discourse norms rather than nihilism.

## 5. Cross-Cutting Themes

### Theme A: The "Anti-PC" Consensus That Isn't

All groups score "today's society is too sensitive about language" above 3.5 except 70s females (3.46). This looks like consensus — but the *reasons* differ:
- **Young males**: frustrated by what they see as pro-feminist or minority-protective speech norms
- **Young females**: frustrated by backlash against their own speech
- **Older groups**: general discomfort with rapid change in discourse norms

The same number, opposite grievances.

### Theme B: Punitive Fairness

"Punishing wrongdoers feels satisfying" scores 3.93 overall — near-universally above neutral. This pairs with high scores for "fair treatment = first principle of legislation." Korean social values are not permissive; **justice is punitive and rule-based**, not rehabilitative.

### Theme C: The Gender Wage Gap — The Sharpest Fracture

Of all 36 questions, the gender wage gap has legitimate reasons shows the largest *within-cohort* gender gap in the 20s (+1.06), and remains the most polarising item overall (mean gender gap: +0.53). This is not merely about gender roles — it reflects fundamentally different empirical beliefs about the cause of inequality.

### Theme D: Equality in Principle, Resistance to Redistribution in Practice

Virtually all groups strongly endorse "equal treatment" and "fair legislation" (4.0+), yet score "everyone earning the same = better world" at only 2.47. Equality is understood as **procedural** (equal rules), not **distributive** (equal outcomes).

### Theme E: Nihilism is Young and Male

Destructive impulse items score highest among young males, though still low in absolute terms:

| Item | 20s M | 70s M | Gap |
|------|-------|-------|-----|
| Society needs to collapse | 2.06 | 1.58 | +0.48 |
| Urge to destroy beautiful things | 1.99 | 1.78 | +0.21 |
| Imagine humanity disappearing | 2.30 | 1.99 | +0.31 |

This is a signal worth watching — not a crisis, but a persistent undercurrent of alienation in young male responses.

## 6. Suggested Reframings

| Original Category | Suggested Label | Rationale |
|-------------------|----------------|-----------|
| 평등 (Equality) | Procedural Equality | Items measure process fairness, not outcome equality |
| 과민성 (Hypersensitivity) | Discourse Norm Resistance | More accurate than pathologising sensitivity |
| 파괴욕구 (Destructive Desire) | Social Alienation | Structural alienation better explains the pattern |
| 보상 (Compensation) | Generational & Class Grievance | Items capture relative deprivation, not just reward |
| 처벌쾌감 (Punitive Pleasure) | Retributive Justice | Less pejorative; reflects a coherent justice philosophy |
